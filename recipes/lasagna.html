Description

Lasagna is a wide, flat sheet of pasta. Lasagna can refer to either the type of noodle or to the typical lasagna dish which is a dish made with several layers of lasagna sheets with sauce and other ingredients, such as meats and cheese, in between the lasagna noodles.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 1/2 lb (225g) ground Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Steps

  1. Cook the Meat Sauce:
    1. Heat a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Add 1 lb ground beef and 1/2 lb ground Italian sausage. Cook until browned, breaking up the meat with a spoon.
    3. Add 1 chopped onion and 3 minced garlic cloves. Cook until the onion is translucent (about 5 minutes).
    4. Stir in 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes, 2 tbsp tomato paste, and 1 can (14 oz) tomato sauce.
    5. Season with 1 tsp sugar, 1 tsp dried basil, 1 tsp dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
    6. Simmer the sauce on low heat for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley toward the end.
  2. Cook the Lasagna Noodles:
    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    2. Add 12 lasagna noodles and cook according to package instructions until al dente.
    3. Drain and lay the noodles flat on a baking sheet to prevent sticking.
  3. Prepare the Ricotta Mixture:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine 16 oz ricotta cheese, 1 large egg, 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley, and 1/2 tsp salt.
    2. Stir until well mixed.
  4. Assemble the Lasagna:
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce at the bottom.
    3. Add a layer of 3-4 lasagna noodles, slightly overlapping.
    4. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
    5. Spoon 1/3 of the meat sauce over the ricotta, and sprinkle 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese.
    6. Repeat the layers two more times (noodles, ricotta, meat sauce, mozzarella).
    7. Finish with a final layer of noodles, remaining meat sauce, and 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese.
  5. Bake the Lasagna:
    1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil (to prevent the cheese from burning).
    2. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
    3. Remove the foil and bake uncovered for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  6. Rest and Serve:
    1. Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes. This helps it set, making it easier to slice.
    2. Garnish with fresh basil if desired, and serve hot.
